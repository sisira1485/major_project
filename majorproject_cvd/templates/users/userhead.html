    Welcome to Face Recognition for Newborns Application

    Biometric recognition of newborns, toddlers, and pre-school children aims is an important research challenge with applications in identifying newborn swapping, missing kids, and disbursing benefits. In this research, we propose a representation learning algorithm to extract unique and invariant features from face images of newborns and toddlers, to design an efficient face recognition algorithm. Specifically, we propose a deep learning model which applies class-based penalties while learning the filters of a convolutional neural network. The proposed CNN architecture achieves a rank-1 identification accuracy of 62.7% for single gallery newborn face recognition and 85.1% for single gallery toddler face recognition, forming state-of-the-results for both the databases. Comparison with several existing algorithms also showcases the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm on both the databases.

    vaccine intake by newborn babies

    Newborn babies typically receive their first vaccination, the hepatitis B vaccine, shortly after birth. Over the following months, they receive a series of vaccinations to protect against various diseases. This includes vaccines such as the rotavirus vaccine, DTaP vaccine (for diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis), Hib vaccine (for Haemophilus influenzae type b), PCV13 vaccine (for pneumococcal disease), IPV vaccine (for polio), and additional doses of some vaccines for booster shots. Around 12 to 15 months of age, they receive vaccines such as the MMR vaccine (for measles, mumps, and rubella) and the varicella vaccine (for chickenpox). Throughout this process, healthcare providers closely monitor the vaccination schedule to ensure timely and appropriate immunization, protecting the health of the child and the community at large.
